\section{The Veil of Darkness Test}
\label{sec:vod}
% ============================================================

\subsection*{Design}

[2–3 paragraphs explaining the VOD logic: officers cannot perceive race in
the dark before making a stop; if daylight increases minority stop rates
(conditional on clock time), this is consistent with racial profiling.
Describe the intertwilight window you use (e.g., 5:00–8:30 PM), the
sunrise/sunset package (R: \texttt{suncalc}; Python: \texttt{astral}), and
the time-of-day bins (e.g., 15-minute dummies).]

\subsection*{Sample Restriction}

[Explain the intertwilight restriction. Report how many stops remain after
restricting to the window. Note whether the VOD sample differs from the
Section~\ref{sec:summary} sample and why.]

% --- Regression specification ---
\subsection*{Specification}

The estimating equation is
\begin{equation}
  \Pr(\text{Minority}_{it} = 1) =
    \beta_0 + \beta_1 \, \text{Daylight}_{it}
    + \sum_{k} \gamma_k \, \mathbf{1}[\text{15-min bin} = k]
    + \varepsilon_{it},
  \label{eq:vod}
\end{equation}
where $\text{Minority}_{it}$ is an indicator equal to one if the stopped
individual is perceived as Black or Hispanic, and $\text{Daylight}_{it}$
equals one if the stop occurred before local sunset.\footnote{
  Sunset times are calculated using [\texttt{suncalc} / \texttt{astral}]
  at the agency's centroid coordinates: [lat, lon].}
All standard errors are heteroskedasticity-robust.

\section{The Outcome Test: Hit Rates}
\label{sec:hitrate}
% ============================================================

\subsection*{Design}

[Explain the outcome-test logic: if officers apply a lower suspicion
threshold to minority drivers, searches of minority drivers should be less
productive (lower hit rate). Describe how you construct the ``any
contraband'' indicator, listing the RIPA columns included.]

% --- Regression specification ---
\subsection*{Specification}

Conditional on a search, the estimating equation is
\begin{equation}
  \Pr(\text{Contraband}_{it} = 1) =
    \alpha_0
    + \alpha_{\text{B}} \, \text{Black}_{it}
    + \alpha_{\text{H}} \, \text{Hispanic}_{it}
    + \varepsilon_{it},
  \label{eq:hit}
\end{equation}
where White is the omitted category. Standard errors are
heteroskedasticity-robust. The sample consists of all stops in which a
search occurred (N = [number of searches]).
